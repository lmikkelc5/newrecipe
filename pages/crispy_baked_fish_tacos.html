Crispy Baked Fish Tacos

1 cup panko breadcrumbs

2 teaspoons chili powder*

1 teaspoon garlic powder

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon fine sea salt

1/2 teaspoon freshly-cracked black pepper

1 egg, whisked

1 1/2 pounds firm white fish, such as cod or halibut, cut into 2-inch pieces

To Make The Fish: Heat oven to 375째F. Spread the panko out in an even layer on a medium baking sheet*. Bake for 5-7 minutes, giving the pan a gentle shake halfway through, until the panko is toasted and golden brown. (Keep a close eye on the panko so that it does not burn.) Transfer the panko to a medium bowl, and dust off the baking sheet for future use.

Add chili powder, garlic powder, cumin, salt, pepper to the bowl with the panko, then whisk the mixture until combined.

Directions

  1. Bake the fish for 10 minutes, or until it is cooked through and opaque and flakes easily with a fork. (Internal temperature should be 145째F*.) Transfer baking sheet to a wire rack, then use a fork to roughly flake the fish into smaller pieces.
  2. *Crispy Panko: To make the panko even crispier, feel free to toss it with 1 tablespoon of oil before toasting it in the oven. I recommend letting the panko toast in the oven until it reaches a pretty deep golden brown for maximum crispiness.
  3. *Fish Internal Temperature: The FDA says that the safe internal temperature for cooked fish is 145째F. I usually pull mine out of the oven around 135-140째F, since the temperature will continue to rise a few degrees more while the fish rests.
