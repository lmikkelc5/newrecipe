Beef Stew

2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1 pound beef or pork stew meat 2 tablespoons cooking oil 3½ cups vegetable juice cocktail ½ cup chopped onion 2 teaspoons instant beef bouillon granules cloves garlic, minced ½ teaspoon dried basil, crushed ½ teaspoon dried thyme, crushed 2¼ cups cubed, peeled potatoes 2 cups sliced carrots 1 cup sliced celery Place flour in a plastic bag. meat to saucepan. Add juice cocktail, onion, bouillon granules, garlic, basil, and thyme. Bring to boiling; reduce heat. HOW TO MAKE THE SOUP There are three parts to making this: the chicken, the gnocchi, and the base of the soup! First up, THE CHICKEN: This is where we get to incorporate a lot of flavor into this soup with some very well-seasoned juicy chicken. We will be using chicken breast and marinating it with the following ingredients: olive oil italian seasoning lemon pepper smoked paprika garlic powder salt black pepper In a bowl, add the chicken breast, olive oil, Italian seasoning, lemon pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Mix to marinade evenly. Heat an oiled skillet or pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook on each side until golden brown, about 4 to 5 minutes each side, depending how thick the breast is. Then drop the heat to low and allow the chicken to cook through to the center or the temperature registers 165°F. Remove the chicken from the pan and allow to cool for 10 minutes. Chop the chicken into cubes now and set aside. If you prefer shredded chicken in the soup instead of cubed, feel free to shred it. SOUP BASE butter olive oil garlic

carrots yellow onion celery chicken broth half and half flour spices parsley basil parmesan Start by sautéing the garlic, chopped carrots, diced onion, and chopped celery in a little bit of butter and oil in a large pot. Cook just until the onions are softened. Then add the chicken broth. Combine the half and half with flour by whisking together in a bowl and then add to the pot. The flour acts as a thickener for the soup just to make it a bit more creamy. Mix all together and then add the following spices to season it up: black pepper, Italian seasoning, sweet paprika, nutmeg, red pepper flakes, and salt. Cover and let it simmer for 30 minutes just for flavors to meld in nicely. After 30 minutes, take off the lid and add parmesan, parsley, red pepper flakes, basil, and the gnocchi. Mix together and let the gnocchi cook for about 9 minutes or until soft. The last step is to toss some spinach in there, taste the soup, adjust any seasoning, and take off the heat to enjoy! To make this even lighter, you can swap the half and half with milk or to make it even more creamy, you can use heavy cream! https://moribyan.com/chicken-gnocchi-soup/

Crispy Baked Fish Tacos

https://www.gimmesomeoven.com/fish-tacos/#tasty-recipes-59652

● 1 cup panko breadcrumbs

● 2 teaspoons chili powder*

● 1 teaspoon garlic powder

● 1 teaspoon ground cumin

● 1 teaspoon fine sea salt

● 1/2 teaspoon freshly-cracked black pepper

● 1 egg, whisked

● 1 1/2 pounds firm white fish, such as cod or halibut, cut into 2-inch pieces

1. To Make The Fish: Heat oven to 375°F. Spread the panko out in an even layer

on a medium baking sheet*. Bake for 5-7 minutes, giving the pan a gentle shake

halfway through, until the panko is toasted and golden brown. (Keep a close eye

on the panko so that it does not burn.) Transfer the panko to a medium bowl, and

dust off the baking sheet for future use.

2. Add chili powder, garlic powder, cumin, salt, pepper to the bowl with the panko,

then whisk the mixture until combined.

3. Now, set up an assembly line with the (1) fish, (2) whisked egg, (3) panko mixture

and (4) parchment-covered baking sheet. Using your right hand, dip a piece of

fish in the egg so that it is coated on all sides. Then, using your left hand,

transfer the fish to the panko mixture and gently press it on so that the fish is

coated on all sides. Using your left hand, transfer the fish to the baking sheet.

Then repeat the process with the remaining fish.

4. Bake the fish for 10 minutes, or until it is cooked through and opaque and flakes

easily with a fork. (Internal temperature should be 145°F*.) Transfer baking

sheet to a wire rack, then use a fork to roughly flake the fish into smaller pieces.

*Crispy Panko: To make the panko even crispier, feel free to toss it with 1 tablespoon of

oil before toasting it in the oven. I recommend letting the panko toast in the oven until it

reaches a pretty deep golden brown for maximum crispiness.

*Fish Internal Temperature: The FDA says that the safe internal temperature for cooked

fish is 145°F. I usually pull mine out of the oven around 135-140°F, since the

temperature will continue to rise a few degrees more while the fish rests.

Crispy Bang Bang Salmon Bites

Ingredients

  • 1. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels to ensure a crispy texture. Remove
  • any skin and cut the salmon into 1-inch bite-sized cubes.
  • 2. Place the salmon pieces in a large bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with
  • garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using).
  • 3. Toss gently to coat the salmon evenly with the seasoning.
  • Step 2: Make the Bang Bang Sauce
  • 1. In a small mixing bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, Thai sweet chili sauce,
  • sriracha, honey, and lime juice until smooth and creamy.
  • 2. Taste and adjust the sauce—add more sriracha for heat or honey for sweetness.
  • Set aside.
  • Step 3: Cook the Salmon
  • 1. Heat a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add a small amount of
  • olive oil to prevent sticking.
  • 2. Once the oil is hot, add the salmon pieces in a single layer, making sure not to
  • overcrowd the pan. Work in batches if needed.
  • 3. Sear the salmon for 2-3 minutes per side, gently turning to brown all sides. Cook
  • until the salmon is golden and crispy on the outside, tender and flaky inside.
  • 4. Transfer cooked salmon bites to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess
  • oil.
  • Step 4: Coat in Bang Bang Sauce Drizzle it: Place the salmon bites on a serving platter and drizzle the sauce over them. Toss it: Add the salmon bites to a bowl, pour the sauce over, and gently toss to coat. Step 5: Garnish and Serve
  • 1. Garnish the salmon bites with chopped parsley, green onions, or sesame seeds
  • for extra flavor and color.
  • 2. Serve immediately to enjoy their crispy texture and vibrant flavors.
  • Tips for Achieving Maximum Crispiness
  • The key to perfectly Crispy Bang Bang Salmon Bites lies in a few simple but essential
  • techniques. Follow these tips to ensure your salmon bites are irresistibly crunchy every
  • time.
  • Pat the Salmon Dry
  • Removing excess moisture is crucial for crispiness. Use a paper towel to thoroughly pat
  • the salmon pieces dry before seasoning. This prevents steaming during cooking and
  • ensures a golden, crispy exterior.
  • Use Cornstarch or Panko Breadcrumbs
  • For an extra crispy coating, lightly dust the salmon pieces with cornstarch before
  • cooking. Alternatively, coat them in panko breadcrumbs for added crunch. Panko’s airy
  • texture creates a delightful crispness that regular breadcrumbs can’t achieve. For more
  • tips on breading techniques, explore this guide.
  • Avoid Overcrowding the Pan or Air Fryer
  • Overcrowding lowers the cooking temperature, leading to unevenly cooked or soggy
  • salmon. Cook in small batches, leaving enough space between pieces to allow air
  • circulation or consistent heat distribution.
  • Maintain the Right Oil Temperature
  • Whether pan-searing or air-frying, ensure the oil is hot enough (around 375°F/190°C)
  • before adding the salmon. Proper oil temperature prevents sticking and sogginess while
  • achieving a crisp outer layer. If the oil isn’t hot enough, the salmon will absorb it, losing
  • its crunch.
