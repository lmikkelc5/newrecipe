French Lentil Rice Soup

6 c chicken broth or vegetable broth 1 c lentils, picked over and rinsed 2 medium carrots, peeled and finely diced 1 small onion, finely chopped 2 celery stalks, finely diced 3 TBS uncooked white rice 2 TBS minced garlic 1 tsp herbes de Provence or dried thyme ½ tsp salt 1/8 tsp ground white or black pepper ¼ c heavy cream or sour cream (optional) ¼ c chopped parsley (optional) Stir together broth, lentils, carrots, onion, celery, rice, garlic, seasoning, salt and pepper in a slow cooker. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours or on high for 4 to 5 hours. Remove 1 ½ c soup and puree in a food processor or blender until almost smooth. Stir pureed soup back into the slow cooker.

Note: Use caution when processing hot liquids in a blender. Vent the blender lid and cover with a clean cloth as directed by manufacturer. Divide soup evenly among 4 serving bowls, garnishing each with cream and parsley if desired. Note: This is the original recipe. I found when I made it that the thyme flavor was too strong. I used 1 ½ times the recipe to make it for my family and I only use ½-3/4 tsp thyme for that amount. I also usually skip the step that calls for pureeing the soup.
