Grandma Lambert’s Molasses Cookies

This recipe is from my great-grandma Marjorie Linton Lambert and these have always been some of my favorite cookies! She was an amazing homemaker and taught my grandma Lambert how to do a lot of the things that were considered essential for running a home (knitting, crocheting and sewing). My grandma has taught me and all of my sisters how to do many of those things and it just adds to my great-grandma Lambert’s legacy. These cookies taste like fall with some of those spices that we love at this time of year and they’re so easy to make. Enjoy!

¾ cup shortening (margarine)

1 cup sugar

1 egg

¼ cup molasses

2 cup flour

1 teaspoon cinnamon

½ teaspoon cloves

½ teaspoon ginger

2 teaspoons baking soda

Make small balls 1 tablespoon, dip in sugar and bake for 8 minutes at 350˚.
